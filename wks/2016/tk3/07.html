Track 3: Beyond

Should We Allow the Government to Spy on Muslim Students?

How Counterterrorism Policies Convey Islamophobia in Public Education Systems

Description

We have witnessed anti-Muslim and xenophobic rhetoric since 9/11, from microaggressions to full-blown racism and violence. Counterterrorism and national security policies have seeped their way into the NYC public education system, affecting students who identity as Arab, Middle Eastern, Muslim, Sikh, and/or South Asian. Join CUNY CLEAR to consider the work AMEMSSA communities are doing in response to the rise of anti-Muslim and xenophobic rhetoric and how you can help dissolve the hate. Students from Brooklyn College will they share their personal stories on discrimination with you in a safe space. Learn how you can help combat Islamophobia during a Know-Your-Rights awareness training section during the workshop to help identify, stand up, and speak out against discrimination, surveillance, and injustice.
