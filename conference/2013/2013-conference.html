NYCAASC: Checkpoints

This April, the New York City Asian American Student Conference celebrates its 10th Anniversary with Checkpoints: Communicating Consciousness.

Join us on 4/23 at the NYU Kimmel Center to discuss A/P/A issues and reflect upon our history, action and inactions.

Register Now! »

En Route

Our theme for this year's conference is EN ROUTE. In past conferences, we examined different notions of Asian American identity and how Asian Americans are reclaiming authorship of their stereotyped representations. This year, we seek to explore how Asian Americans are reinventing themselves by moving beyond their own communities and contributing to an intercultural dialogue.

Keynote Speaker + Performances

Jeff Chang, Keynote Speaker

HELEN ZIA is an award-winning journalist, Fulbright Scholar, and former Executive Editor of Ms. Magazine. Named as one of the most influential Asian Americans of the decade by A. Magazine, her articles, essays, and reviews have appeared in numerous publications, books and anthologies. She has covered social and political movements of the Asian American community for the past thirty years. Her range of work is deeply rooted in civil rights advocacy, as noted in her involvement in the landmark anti-Asian violence Vincent Chin case of the 1980s. Zia is also the author of Asian American Dreams: The Emergence of an American People and the co-author of My Country Versus Me. President Bill Clinton quoted from Asian American Dreams at two separate speeches in the Rose Garden.

As a second generation Chinese American, Zia has been outspoken on issues ranging from civil rights and peace to women's rights and countering hate violence and homophobia. In 1997 she co-authored a complaint to the U.S. Commission on Civil Rights and testified at a commission hearings on the impact of bias in the campaign finance investigations. Zia has appeared in numerous news programs and films; her work on the landmark civil rights case of anti-Asian violence which is documented in the Academy Award nominated film, "Who Killed Vincent Chin?" She was profiled in Bill Moyers' PBS documentary, "Becoming American: The Chinese Experience."

An out-lesbian, Helen and her spouse were married during the gay weddings that took place in San Francisco in 2004 and again in 2008. Helen's affidavit on behalf of marriage equality was part of the successful appeal to the California Supreme Court; in 2010, Helen testified as a witness in the landmark federal lawsuit by Olsen/Boies, Perry v. Schwarzenegger. After schooling, she went to work as a construction laborer, an autoworker, and a community organizer, after which she discovered her life's work as a writer. She holds an honorary Doctor of Laws degree from the Law School of the City University of New York.

Hari Kondabolu, Comic

HARI KONDABOLU is a Brooklyn-based, Queens-raised comic who has been described by Time Out NY as "smart, analytical and rising." He has performed on Jimmy Kimmel Live, Comedy Central's Live at Gotham and John Oliver's New York Standup Show and the 2007 HBO U.S. Comedy Arts Festival. His Comedy Central Presents half-hour television special debuted on the network in February 2011.

Hayoo, Talent Show Performer

HAYOO () is an acoustic duet consisting of John Chang (SEAS 14) and Esther Kim (CC14). Formed last year, they have performed at various events at Columbia University including Lowlapalooza, Live at Lerner, and the KSA culture show. They are excited to be here today, and hope you enjoy the show!

Johnny Li Talent Show Performer

JOHNNY LI graduated from Cornell University in 2012, majoring in Human Development. He is passionate about hip hop music and fashion, and has freelanced at GO. He now works at Barney's as a men's buyer.

Austin Luu, Talent Show Performer

AUSTIN LUU is a 19 year old Chinese-American singer-songwriter originally from Bethel, CT but now resides in Queens, NY. He attends Queens College where he is on a path to become a schoolteacher. His goal is to release an acoustic EP in the near future with a sound that is highly influenced by Indie, Pop, and R&B. If you would like to stay updated on his upcoming events, music, etc. Check him out at his Youtube (Aluumusic) his Facebook page (AustinLuuMusic) and follow him on Twitter (@Austin_Luu)

Nisha Parikh, Talent Show Performer

NISHA PARIKH is currently a rising junior at NYU's Stern School of Business majoring in Finance and Pre-Medical Studies. She has launched a Global Arts Education Program, Glance, where she helped students explore different cultures through civic involvement and dance. Nisha is also a Bolly-wood dance teacher and has taught for multiple organizations, including Groove with Me, Women Aware, Satrangi School of Fusion, and PS 129. Currently, she is the Internal Vice President of NYU Phi Chi Theta Business Fraternity, and Internal Vice President of the Indian Cultural Exchange.

Beatrice "Bea" Go, NYCAASC 2012 Talent Show Winner

BEATRICE "BEA" GO is a 19-year-old singer from Westchester, NY. Formerly a visual arts student, she's recently decided to pursue her passion for music full-time. Her first solo performance in front of an audience was at last year's NYCAASC talent show, where she won first place! Shortly after, she was selected as a final vocal competitor for Kollaboration New York's 2012 show. On her YouTube channel she has an audience of over 10,000 subscribers! Driven and determined, her ultimate goal is to inspire people who were once like her, who kept their dreams in the closet, to believe in themselves enough to follow their passions.
