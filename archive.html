Past conferences

2016 - Checkpoints: Communicating Consciousness

The tenth annual NYCAASC, titled Checkpoints: Communicating Consciousness, challenged attendees to confront and overcome existing boundaries between individuals, families, and communities.

2015 - Critical Mass

The ninth annual NYCAASC theme was Critical Mass, which focused on how Asian/Pacific Americans can be better understood in the sharp reflections of past and current events.

2014 - Roots

The eighth annual NYCAASC theme was Roots. Following the path of the previous year’s theme, En Route, Roots encapsulates refocusing on the past as the key to our future.

2013 - En Route

The seventh annual NYCAASC theme was En Route which explored how Asian Americans are reinventing themselves by moving beyond their own communities and contributing to an intercultural dialogue.

2012 - INK: Imagine and Rethink

The sixth annual conference was titled INK: Imagine and Rethink which explored the notions of Asian American identity and how mainstream representation encroach on definitions of self.

2011 - Emerging Profiles

The fifth annual conference was themed Emerging Profiles which explored the ways in which the Internet and media have shaped today’s discussions about the Asian American identity.

2010 - Change in Motion

The fourth annual NYCAASC was titled Change in Motion and explored the movement and changes in Asian America, examining the past to inform the present and change the future.

2009 - Act: Empowering Our Generation

The third conference was themed Act: Empowering Our Generation. This conference focused on mobilizing individuals and communities to challenge and transform injustices faced by Asian Americans.

2008 - Redefine

The theme for the second conference was Redefine, in which attendees explored the evolution of the Asian American community and were asked to redefine what it means to be Asian American.

2007 - Break the Silence

NYCAASC began as a collaboration between New York University and Columbia University. The first conference was themed Break the Silence: Discover, Inspire, Empower.
